About

I’m a designer, builder, and strategic design leader. Across everything I do, I’m most energized by turning fragile ideas into products, systems, and stories that people deeply connect with.

For over 20 years, I’ve helped organizations navigate moments of technological change, shaping early-stage ideas and mature products into experiences people trust and love. My work spans product, brand, and systems design — from crafting Netflix’s custom typeface and interface innovations, to defining early agentic capabilities at Google Assistant, to guiding Google Photos through years of sustained growth and visual evolution.

Today, my focus is AI.

At Google Photos, I work at the intersection of product strategy, design, and emerging AI capabilities. I design and prototype with generative and agentic systems, increasingly using code as a design medium to explore, communicate, and ship new interaction models enabled by AI.

Alongside this work, I lead cross-organizational initiatives focused on AI-native ways of working. I build prototyping frameworks, connect designers to production AI infrastructure, and build systems that help design and product teams develop new skills, practices, and mental models for an AI-first future.

Throughout my career, I’ve led teams, built organizations, and helped executives navigate ambiguity by turning complex problems into clear product direction. Today, I apply those same skills to helping teams understand and adapt to one of the most significant technology shifts of our time.

Selected Work

Designing Trust at Scale — Leading Growth at Google Photos

Led growth design at Google Photos, managing backup, storage, onboarding, and visual evolution. Guided the transition to Material 3 Expressive, aligning teams and execs around a clearer, more emotionally resonant product — contributing to sustained, multi-year growth and elevated user trust.

blog.google/products-and-platforms/platforms/android/material-3-expressive-android-wearos-launch

Reframing Productivity as Emotional Clarity — Not Just Tasks

At Google Assistant, I led a team exploring AI-powered delegation, memory, and proactive coordination. We grounded the work in human clarity, shaping a design strategy that influenced cross-functional investments and laid foundations for agentic capabilities like awareness, orchestration, and emotionally intelligent assistance.

blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/assistant/ces-2020-google-assistant

Designing the friction out of Netflix

It’s hard to find something to “partly watch” on Netflix. So, I led a team of four designers who set out to answer ‘How might we make it easier to choose something to watch, when you don’t want to pay attention?’.

medium.com/@misterdavidgallagher/designing-the-friction-out-of-netflix techcrunch.com/2020/08/18/netflix-test-puts-a-shuffle-play-button-right-on-your-home-screen

Cultivating Empathy to Drive Product Strategy

I traveled across the US, Europe & Asia with my research, product and engineering partners to meet members and cultivate empathy within Netflix. We produced 12 films about innovation opportunities across our web, TV and mobile platforms and brought them to our colleagues in immersive, physical spaces at our Los Gatos HQ.

medium.com/@misterdavidgallagher/cultivating-empathy-to-drive-product-strategy

Designing a Custom Typeface

I was part of the core team who, in collaboration with Dalton Maag, designed Netflix Sans. A contemporary grotesque typeface, inspired by our cinematic heritage and innovation-focussed culture. It’s designed to be legible and space efficient on low-end devices in emerging markets while standing confident on billboards on Hollywood Boulevard.

itsnicethat.com/news/netflix-sans-typeface-dalton-maag

Designing a personalized BBC

In 2012, I built and led a team of 8 designers to articulate a future vision of how the BBC could use personalization to serve different content, in different ways for different people. My team’s work was used to begin the technical feasibility of personalization services and inform how the corporation would think about new content architectures. The first step of this multi-part strategy rolled out in 2015.

medium.com/@misterdavidgallagher/designing-a-personalized-bbc

User Centered Design for a Physical Space

In 2014, I led an initiative to transform the 5th floor of the flagship BBC building in Manchester, UK. As Design Director, I built a team who facilitated a deeply user-centered approach to the project and led the interior design of the space. We launched the “Blueroom”, on time and under budget — a space that brings BBC audiences and future technologies to journalists, engineers, designers, product managers and the wider BBC team to inform the content and programs they make.

medium.com/@misterdavidgallagher/ucd-for-a-physical-space

Designing Internet TV for BBC Three

In Summer 2014, I joined a multidisciplinary team from across the BBC to define a strategy and vision that would transform the BBC Three TV channel into an Internet TV service. And in 2015 I built and led a team of 5 designers to design the multi-platform product.

medium.com/@misterdavidgallagher/designing-internet-tv-for-bbc-three
